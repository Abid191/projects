Blood Donation

  1. Eligibility check:

    Potential donors are evaluated to determine if they meet the criteria for donating blood.

  2. Health history review:

    Donors are asked to provide information about their health history, including any medications they are taking and recent illnesses.

  3. Blood draw:

    A medical professional draws blood from the donor's arm and collects it in a sterile bag.

  4. Refreshments:

    After the blood donation, the donor is given refreshments and a chance to rest to help the body replace the lost blood.

  5. Testing:

    The collected blood is tested for various diseases and conditions to ensure it is safe for transfusion.

  6. Transfusion:

    The collected blood is then transfused to someone in need, either directly or by being added to the blood bank for future use.

Note: Before donating blood, it is important to drink plenty of fluids and eat a healthy meal to ensure the body has enough resources to replace the blood.
